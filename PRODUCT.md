# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Style-conscious people who document outfits, want to understand their personal aesthetic over time, and enjoy discovering other people with a similar visual language. The primary mobile workflow is capturing a full-body outfit, receiving a DNA reading, saving it as a collectible trace, and browsing friends, twins, and past looks.

## Product Purpose

outft. turns everyday outfit photos into an evolving visual archive and style identity. Success means capture feels immediate, the result feels worth keeping, social browsing remains image-led, and users can recognize patterns across their wardrobe without reading a conventional analytics dashboard.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, intimate, collectible. The product should feel like a fashion contact sheet crossed with a personal receipt archive: quiet, tactile, and specific rather than glossy or trend-chasing.

## Anti-references

- Generic social feeds where a small thumbnail sits beside a username and metadata.
- SaaS dashboards, analytics cards, bright gradients, glass effects, and excessive pills.
- Landscape outfit crops that hide the full silhouette.
- Dense profile grids without the physical character of polaroids, receipts, contact sheets, or handwritten numbering.
- Decorative motion or novelty interactions that slow down capture and browsing.

## Design Principles

1. The outfit is always the dominant object.
2. Every captured trace should feel collectible and worth revisiting.
3. DNA explains identity visually before it explains it numerically.
4. Social context belongs beneath the image, with comments and reactions available in place.
5. Navigation stays predictable and reachable with one thumb.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and keyboard access, maintain 44-pixel touch targets, provide text alternatives for meaningful imagery, support reduced motion, and never use color alone to communicate state.
